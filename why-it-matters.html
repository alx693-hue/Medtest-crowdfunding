The Chemical Breakdown

When IV solutions expire, chemical decomposition occurs:

  • Active ingredients degrade into inactive or toxic compounds
  • Plasticizers from IV bags leach into solutions
  • Sterility cannot be guaranteed after expiration
  • pH levels change, affecting drug stability
Real Incident: In 2018, a Houston hospital administered expired saline solutions to 12 patients, causing adverse reactions.

Look-Alike, Sound-Alike Errors

Common mix-ups in IV medications:

  • Epinephrine vs. Ephedrine (Cardiac risk)
  • Insulin vs. Heparin (Dosing errors)
  • Potassium Chloride mistaken for saline
  • Similar packaging causing visual confusion
Statistics: Look-alike medications contribute to 25% of all medication errors (ISMP).

Unintended Chemical Changes

Drugs can change during storage or transport:

  • Temperature fluctuations cause chemical reactions
  • Light exposure leads to photodegradation
  • Oxidation from air exposure in IV lines
  • Interaction with IV tubing materials
Example: Nitroglycerin loses 50% potency after 24 hours in PVC IV bags.
